\question The radius $R$ of a sphere is increasing at a constant rate of 
 $\vi$ cm/sec. 

 \watchout

\begin{parts}
  \part[3] When $R=\vr$ cm, then how fast is the volume of the sphere increasing?

  \begin{solution}[\mcq]
    The volume of a sphere is given by 
    \[ V = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi R^3 \]
    Which means,
    \begin{align}
      \dfrac{\ud V}{\ud t} &= \dfrac{4}{3}\pi\cdot(3R^2)\cdot\dfrac{\ud R}{\ud t} = 4\pi\cdot R^2\dfrac{\ud R}{\ud t}
    \end{align}
    And therefore, when $R=\vr$ cm, then 
    \begin{align}
      \dfrac{\ud V}{\ud t}&= 4\pi\cdot(\vr\text{ cm})^2\times\vi\text{ cm/s} \\
          &= \va\pi\text{ cubic cm/sec} 
    \end{align}
  \end{solution}

  \part[3] When the volume of the sphere is $V=\WRITEFRAC[false]\vv\vw\pi$ cubic cm, then how fast is its surface area increasing?

  \begin{solution}[\mcq]
    \begin{align}
      V &= \dfrac{4}{3}\pi\cdot R^3 = \WRITEFRAC[false]\vv\vw\pi \\
      \implies R^3 &= \b\text{ cubic cm}\implies R = \vd\text{ cm}
    \end{align}
    The surface area $S$ of a sphere with radius $R$ is 
     \[ S = 4\pi\cdot R^2 \] 
     And therefore, at $R=\vd\text{ cm}$ and $\dfrac{\ud R}{\ud t}=\vi\text{ cm/s}$, 
     \[ \dfrac{\ud S}{\ud t} = 4\pi\dfrac{\ud R^2}{\ud t} = 8\pi\cdot R\dfrac{\ud R}{\ud t} = \vb\pi\text{ sq. cm/sec} \]
  \end{solution}

  \part[3] At what value of radius $R$ would the radius be changing as fast as the volume in increasing? 

  \begin{solution}[\mcq]
    Basically, we have to find the $R$ at which 
    \begin{align}
      \dfrac{\ud V}{\ud t} &= \dfrac{\ud R}{\ud t} \\ 
      \implies 4\pi\cdot R^2\dfrac{\ud R}{\ud t} &= \dfrac{\ud R}{\ud t} \\
      \implies R^2 &= \dfrac{1}{4\pi}\text{ or } R = \dfrac{1}{2\sqrt\pi}\text{ cm}
    \end{align}
  \end{solution}
\end{parts}
