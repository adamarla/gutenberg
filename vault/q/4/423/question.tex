\question Given the word \text{\vw }, how many words (with or without meaning) can be formed that $\ldots$ 

\watchout

\begin{parts}
  \part $\ldots$ begin with an E? 

  \begin{solution}
    The word \text{\vw } has $\vn$ characters with no character repeating. 

    And hence, if E is fixed as the first character, then the remaining $\a$ characters
    can be permuted in
    \[ N = \a! = \vi\text{ ways}\] 
  \end{solution}

  \part $\ldots$ begin with an E but end with an A?

  \begin{solution}
    With two characters fixed (first and last), only the middle $\b$ characters need to be permuted. 
    And hence, 
    \[ N = \b! = \vj \]
  \end{solution}

  \part $\ldots$ have all the vowels together? 

  \begin{solution}
    Of the $\vn$ characters in \text{\vw }, $\vc$ are consonants and $\vv$ are vowels. 

    If we \textbf{bundle} the vowels together into one unit, then in all we will have 
    \[ N = \underbrace{\vc}_{\text{consonants}} + \underbrace{1}_{\text{vowel bundle}} = \c \] 
    entities to permute. This can happen in $\c!$ ways.

    Moreover, the \textbf{vowels can permute} within the bundle in $\vv!$ ways. 

    And hence, 
      \[ \text{Answer}=\c!\times\vv! = \vk \]
  \end{solution}

\end{parts}
