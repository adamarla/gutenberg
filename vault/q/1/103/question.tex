\question Fuel expenditures for a steamship are propotional to the cube of it speed.
It is known that at a speed of $\vs$ km/hour, fuel costs are \$ $\vf$ /hour. Other 
expenses (independent of speed) amount to \$ $\vo$ per hour. 

\watchout

\begin{parts}
  \part[4] At what speed should the ship travel to keep its total expenses 
  \textbf{per kilometer} lowest?

\begin{solution}[\halfpage]
  If $F(s)$ be the fuel expenditure \textbf{per hour}, then 
  \[ F(s) = k\cdot s^3,\text{ where } s = \text{ speed in km/h} \]
  Moreover, 
  \[ F(\vs) = \vf\text{ \$/h} = 
  k\cdot(\vs\text{ km/h})^3\implies 
  k = \dfrac\p\q\dfrac{\$\text{/h}}{(\text{km/h})^3}\]
  The \textbf{total expenditure/hour} would therefore be,
  \[ T(s) = F(s) + \vo = \left(\dfrac\p\q\cdot s^3 + \vo\right)\$\text{/h} \]
  Now, if $K(s)$ be the \textbf{total expenditure per km}, then 
  \[ K(s) = T(s)\,\$\text{/h}\times\dfrac{1}{s}\text{ h/km} = \dfrac\vo{s} + \dfrac\p\q\cdot s^2  \]
  And for $K(s)$ to be minimum,
  \begin{align}
    \dfrac{\ud}{\ud s}K(s) &= -\dfrac\vo{s^2} + \dfrac\vb\vc\cdot s = 0 \\
    \implies s^3 &= \WRITEFRAC\a\b\implies s = \vm\text{ km/h} \\
    \text{and }\dfrac{\ud^2}{\ud s^2}K(s) &= \dfrac\vp{s^3} + \dfrac\vb\vc > 0\text{ (also true)} 
  \end{align}
  Hence, the ship will incur the \textbf{lowest cost per km} when 
  \[ s = \vm\text{ km/h}\] 

\end{solution}

\part[1] What will the total expenditure \textbf{per hour} at that speed be? 

\begin{solution}[\mcq]
    The total expenditure per hour would be 
    \[ T(\vm) = \dfrac\p\q\cdot\vm^3 + \vo = \WRITEFRAC\vv\vw\,\$\text{/h} \] 
  \end{solution}

\end{parts}
