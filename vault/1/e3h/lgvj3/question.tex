\question[3] In the figure alongside, $PQRS$ is the diameter of a circle with radius $\va$ cm.
The lengths $PQ$, $QR$ and $RS$ are equal. Semi-circles are drawn on $PQ$ and $QS$ as diameters.
Find the area of the shaded region.

\begin{solution}[\halfpage]
	\begin{align}
		PS &= 2\times OP = \vd\text{ cm} \\ 
		\therefore PQ &= QR = RS \implies PQ = QR = RS = \vp\text{ cm}
	\end{align}
	
	And hence, the required area $A$ is
	\begin{align}
		A &= \dfrac{1}{2}\pi\dfrac{PS^2}{4} 
		- \dfrac{1}{2}\pi\dfrac{QS^2}{4} 
    + \dfrac{1}{2}\pi\dfrac{PQ^2}{4} \\ 
		&= \dfrac{\pi}{8}\cdot\left( \vd^2 - \vq^2 + \vp^2\right) = \WRITEFRAC\vm{8}\pi\text{ cm}^2
	\end{align}
\end{solution}
