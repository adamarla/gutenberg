\begin{skill}
\begin{narrow}
\textcolor{blue}{Integrals \#4}

Trickier integrals. For example, 

\small$\int \dfrac{dx}{\sqrt{ax^2+bx+c}}, \int \dfrac{px+q}{ax^2+bx+c}\cdot dx$ \normalsize

\end{narrow}

\reason 

The table below summarizes the strategy to use when one encounters integrals of the following forms 

\begin{center}
  \begin{tabular}{NN}
  \toprule 
        \text{Integral} & \text{Convert to}  \\
   \midrule 
   \int \frac{dx}{\sqrt{ax^2+bx+c}}\cdot dx & \int \frac{dx}{\sqrt{\left(x + \alpha \right)^2 \pm \beta^2}}\\
    \midrule 
    \int \frac{dx}{ax^2+bx+c}\cdot dx & \int \frac{dx}{\left(x + \alpha \right)^2 \pm \beta^2}\\
    \midrule 
    \int\frac{ \left( px+q\right) \cdot dx}{\sqrt{ax^2+bx+c}} & \underbrace{ \int \frac{A f'(x) + B}{\sqrt{f(x)}}\cdot dx}_{f(x) = ax^2+bx+c}\\
    \midrule
        \int\frac{ \left( px+q\right) \cdot dx}{ax^2+bx+c} & \underbrace{ \int \frac{A f'(x) + B}{f(x)}\cdot dx}_{f(x) = ax^2+bx+c}\\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{center}

So, these might be four different types of integrals. But they can be solved using jsut two strategies \newline 

\begin{center}
\begin{itemize}
\item{Completing the squares, that is, re-writing the \underline{denominator} $f(x) = ax^2+bx+c$ as $ \left(x+\alpha \right)^2 \pm \beta$}
\item{Re-writing the \underline{numerator} as $Af'(x)+B$, where $f(x)$ is the denominator }
\end{itemize}

\end{center}
