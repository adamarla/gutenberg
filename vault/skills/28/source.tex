\textbf{Basic Identities} 

The following trigonometric identities 
are fundamental \newline 

We are not giving proofs for them here. But you should
\underline{commit these to memory}

\smallmath
\begin{align}
\sin (A+B) &= \sin A\cdot \cos B + \cos A\cdot \sin B \\ 
\sin (A-B) &= \sin A\cdot \cos B - \cos A\cdot \sin B  \\
\cos (A + B) &= \cos A \cdot \cos B - \sin A\cdot \sin B \\
\cos (A-B) &= \cos A\cdot \cos B + \sin A\cdot \sin B \\ 
\tan (A+B) &= \dfrac{\tan A + \tan B}{1-\tan A\cdot \tan B} \\
\tan (A-B) &= \dfrac{\tan A - \tan B}{1+ \tan A\cdot \tan B} 
\end{align}

\end{skill}

\textbf{Derieved Identities} 

The following identities are \underline{specific cases} of the identities above 


\smallmath
\begin{align}
\sin 2A &= \sin (A+A) = 2\sin A\cdot \cos A  \\
\sin 3A &= \sin (2A + A) = 3\sin A - 4\sin^3 A \\ 
\cos 2A &= \cos (A+A) = \cos^2 A - \sin^2 A  \\
\cos 3A &= 4\cos^3 A - 3\cos A \\ 
\tan 2A &= \dfrac{2\tan A}{1-\tan^2 A}  \\
\tan 3A &= \dfrac{3\tan A - \tan^3 A}{1-3\tan^2 A} 
\end{align}
