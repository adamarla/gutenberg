\textcolor{blue}{Common Derivatives}

Derivatives of powers, logarithmic \&\\
trigonometric functions etc.

\reason 

%text
You would encounter the following
derivatives most frequently in 
your studies. The proofs are easy. 
But we will just give the result\newline 

\textbf{Note: } In the table below, $\log x$
is actually \underline{$\log$ to the base $e$}

%
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{MM}
\toprule
f(x) & \frac{d}{dx}f(x) \\ 
\midrule
x^n,\, n\in\mathbb{R}  & n\cdot x^{n-1} \\
\midrule 
e^x & e^x \\
\midrule 
a^x & \log a \cdot a^x \\
\midrule 
\log x & \frac{1}{x} \\ 
\midrule 
\sin x & \cos x \\
\midrule 
\cos x & -\sin x \\ 
\midrule 
\tan x & \sec^2 x \\
\midrule 
\sin^{-1} x,\,x\in(-1,1) & \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \\
\midrule 
\cos^{-1} x, \, x\in(-1,1) & -\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \\
\midrule 
\tan^{-1} x & \dfrac{1}{1+x^2} \\
\bottomrule 
\end{tabular} 
\end{center}
