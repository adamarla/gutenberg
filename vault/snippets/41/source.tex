\begin{snippet}
    
    \incorrect
    
    $f(x) = x - \log x$ is defined for all $x\in\mathbb{R}$ 
    
    \reason
    
    \begin{center}
  \begin{tabular}{NNN}
   \toprule
        & \text{Not defined if} & \text{Domain} \\
   \midrule 
   x & & \mathbb{R} \\
    \midrule 
    \log x & x \leq 0 & \mathbb{R}^+ \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{center}

The domain of $f(x)$ is the \underline{stricter} of the two domains listed above\newline 

Hence, the domain is $\mathbb{R}^+ \text{ or } x > 0$
    
\end{snippet}
